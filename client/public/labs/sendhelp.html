Send help,
not flowers.

Your mom will never ask. The new parents say "we're fine." The friend after surgery insists on managing. Flowers wilt on the counter of a house that actually needs a cleaner, a lawn mowed, dinner-hour babysitting. Help is the gift everyone needs and nobody requests — so we made it sendable. With their dignity doing the steering.

1 · Who needs a hand?

2 · What do you send?

3 · Say it in your own hand

4 · Split it with the family — one tap each

The part that makes it work

They stay in charge. Always. Your gift arrives as an invitation, not an appointment. They accept it (or quietly decline), pick the times, meet the pro first if they like, and can convert it to any service they prefer. Nobody gets a surprise stranger, and nobody feels managed. It reads as love, not as a verdict on their housekeeping.
